Model Architecture

Framework: LASSO + XGBoost + SHAP

Validation: 270 cases (30% hold-out) + 2,847 MIMIC-IV external
Feature Selection: LASSO regression (lambda=0.0049) + Boruta confirmation

What is SHAP?

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is a game-theoretic approach to explain machine learning predictions. It assigns each feature an importance value for a particular prediction. Red (↑) = increases FI risk; Blue (↓) = decreases FI risk.

LOW RISK FI Probability < 30%

  • Daily nutritional risk assessment and documentation
  • Head-of-bed elevation at 30-45 degrees
  • Standard EN protocol: start 20-30 mL/h, increase 10-20 mL/h every 8-12h
  • Monitor gastric residual volume every 4-6 hours
  • Pause or reduce EN rate if GRV > 250 mL
  • Oral care 2-3 times per day; Daily EN tolerance evaluation

MODERATE RISK FI Probability 30-60%

All standard care + enhanced interventions:

  • Low-dose EN initiation (10-15 mL/h) with feeding pump
  • Prokinetic agents: metoclopramide 10mg tid or erythromycin 200mg bid
  • Monitor intra-abdominal pressure every 4-6 hours
  • Consider switching to pre-digested formula if GRV elevated
  • Blood glucose monitoring (target 6-10 mmol/L)
  • Weekly serum albumin and prealbumin monitoring

HIGH RISK FI Probability > 60%

All standard + enhanced care + intensive management:

  • Emergency NST (Nutrition Support Team) consultation
  • Hourly gastric residual monitoring (first 72 hours)
  • Continuous intra-abdominal pressure monitoring
  • EN start at 10-15 mL/h, increase by 5-10 mL/h every 12-24h
  • Multi-modal prokinetic combination therapy
  • Daily MDT rounds; Consider supplemental PN if target not met by day 3

Limitations & Disclaimers

Clinical Limitations

  • Performance may vary in different clinical settings not represented in training data.
  • Individual patient characteristics beyond the 10 predictors may influence FI risk.
  • The model does not account for temporal parameter changes during ICU stay.

Technical Notes
